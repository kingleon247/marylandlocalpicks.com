# Print Drop Operations Notes

These are planning notes, not a substitute for current printer and USPS rules.
Verify rates, dimensions, route counts, paperwork, bundling, and drop-off
instructions before every mailing.

## Working Launch Format

- Market: Catonsville, Maryland
- Card: 9x12 working format
- Quantity: roughly 2,500 households
- Delivery: USPS Every Door Direct Mail
- Cadence: approximately every six weeks
- Sizes: half, single, double, and quad
- Payment: paid before a print spot is reserved

EDDM quantities follow full carrier routes, so the final piece count may not be
an exact round target.

## Operating Cycle

1. Select the city, routes, capacity, theme, and sales deadline.
2. Sell placements and collect payment before production.
3. Collect logos, photos, contact details, services, hours, and offers.
4. Build landing pages and test QR destinations.
5. Design the card and collect written proof approvals.
6. Verify every QR code, phone number, URL, offer, and expiration date.
7. Confirm print stock, bleed, shipping, timing, and EDDM eligibility.
8. Prepare route paperwork, facing slips, bundles, and drop-off logistics.
9. Mail, notify advertisers, monitor activity, and begin renewals.

## Quality Gates

Before print:

- Every paid or intentional house placement is accounted for
- Business details and offer terms are verified
- Advertisers have approved proofs
- QR codes are tested on a real phone
- Mailing panel, indicia, margins, and orientation are approved
- Quantity matches the selected carrier routes

After mailing:

- Send advertisers live-page links and card photos
- Retain acceptance or postage records
- Follow up after 7-14 days
- Start renewal conversations before reopening category inventory
- Offer digital-only continuation when print is not renewed

## Core Rule

Sell first, then produce. Do not commit to print and postage until paid
placements cover direct costs and justify the operator time.
