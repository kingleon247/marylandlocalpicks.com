## Page 4: Advertiser Products, Packages, Pricing Logic, Payment Options, and Renewal Strategy

### 1. Purpose of This Document

This document defines the Maryland Local Picks offer menu.

It is designed for internal use, future team training, sales planning, and business model clarity.

The goal is to make the product simple to sell while still supporting multiple revenue streams.

Maryland Local Picks should not be positioned as “just a postcard.”

The real offer is:

**Print exposure + digital landing page + local offer placement + QR traffic + renewal opportunities + future marketing services.**

The printed card is the first product, but the larger business is built from:

* repeat print placements
* digital-only listings
* paid Pick of the Week features
* social promotion
* call tracking
* AI answering later
* website upgrades
* review/reputation services
* digital advertising
* recurring local business relationships

### 2. Core Sales Positioning

The basic advertiser message:

**“We’re putting together Catonsville Local Picks — a printed card and digital guide featuring selected local businesses, offers, and places worth knowing. The card goes to local households, and every paid print advertiser also gets a digital landing page with a QR code so people can view your business and offer online.”**

This should feel:

* local
* curated
* community-based
* useful to residents
* simple for the business owner
* more premium than a coupon sheet
* more tangible than digital ads alone

Avoid language that makes it sound like:

* a cheap coupon book
* a generic ad mailer
* a marketing agency cold pitch
* a directory no one visits
* a complicated tech product

The clean framing:

**“You are getting featured in a local print-and-digital guide.”**

### 5. Firm Pricing (Current)

Current firm package prices per 10,000-home mailing:

* Half Spot — $350
* Standard Spot — $600
* Double Spot — $1,100

Do not use old test pricing ($150, $250, $300, $450, $500, $800, or other previous amounts).

The business sells local household reach, design coordination, category scarcity, and done-for-you placement — not guaranteed leads.

### 6. Launch Pricing Strategy

For the first card, pricing can be positioned as an introductory launch rate.

This helps close early advertisers without making the brand feel cheap.

Example language:

**“Since this is the first Catonsville edition, I’m offering founding advertiser pricing for the launch card. After the first card is out and we have the printed sample and digital pages live, pricing may increase for future editions.”**

This gives a reason for the lower first-card price and creates urgency.

Possible launch structure:

* half spot: entry price
* standard spot: core launch price
* double spot: premium launch price
* quad spot: sponsor launch price

Do not make the launch price so low that the work is not worth doing.

The first card should still cover costs and create profit.

### 7. Recurring Every-Six-Weeks Print Plan

Because the target cadence is one mailing approximately every six weeks, one recurring print option should exist.

This is for businesses that want to stay visible every edition.

Positioning:

**“We’re planning to mail roughly every six weeks. If you want to stay in front of the same local area consistently, you can reserve your spot on each edition instead of deciding one card at a time.”**

Includes:

* print spot every edition
* ongoing digital landing page
* ability to update offer each mailing
* renewal priority
* basic reporting
* locked rate while active
* optional category lock depending on package

This plan should be billed automatically or prepaid.

Use for:

* HVAC
* plumbing
* roofing
* landscaping
* Realtor
* insurance
* dentist
* med spa
* senior care
* auto repair
* high-ticket services

This creates predictable revenue and makes future drops easier to fill.

### 8. Prepaid Multi-Mailing Packages

#### One Mailing

#### Three-Mail Package

Best for:

### 9. Discount vs Bonus Strategy

Avoid heavy discounts.

Discounting too much creates problems:

* lowers perceived value
* makes future price increases harder
* attracts weaker advertisers
* makes the work less profitable
* trains businesses to wait for a deal

Use bonuses instead.

Better bonuses:

* Pick of the Week feature
* social promotion
* landing page upgrade
* priority placement
* category priority
* extra offer update
* quarterly report
* added QR tracking
* first right of refusal
* locked rate
* free design refresh
* digital-only continuation included for a period

Example:

Instead of:

**“Pay for 3 and get 20% off.”**

Use:

**“Pay for 3 and get your rate locked, priority placement, and one Pick of the Week feature included.”**

This keeps value high.

### 10. Digital-Only Listing

Digital-only is both an entry product and a downsell.

It should be lower-cost than print.

Best for businesses that:

* cannot afford print
* are not ready for the card
* missed the print deadline
* want to test the platform
* do not need household mail exposure
* stopped printing but should not churn completely

Includes:

* business landing page
* listing on city page
* current offer/coupon
* basic tap tracking
* QR/link shareability
* possible Pick of the Week eligibility

Does not include:

* print card placement
* mailed household exposure
* guaranteed premium placement
* call tracking unless added
* social promotion unless added

Positioning:

**“If the print card is not the right fit right now, we can still keep your business listed digitally with a landing page and offer.”**

This is the “save the deal” product.

When a print advertiser does not renew:

**“No problem. Instead of coming off completely, we can keep your digital page and offer active for a lower monthly rate.”**

### 13. Call Tracking Add-On

Call tracking is a premium add-on.

Basic advertisers get call-button tap tracking.

Premium advertisers get a SignalWire tracking number.

#### Basic Call Tap Tracking

Included with landing pages.

Tracks:

* someone tapped the Call button

Does not prove:

* call connected
* call lasted
* business answered
* lead quality

Report as:

**call taps**

Not:

**calls**

#### SignalWire Call Tracking

Premium add-on.

A SignalWire number appears on the landing page and forwards to the business.

Tracks:

* real calls
* caller number if available
* timestamp
* call duration
* call status if available
* recording link if recording is enabled properly

Positioning:

**“If you want more proof than call-button taps, we can give you a dedicated tracking number and call report.”**

Best for:

* digital-only advertisers
* high-ticket service businesses
* businesses buying multiple mailings
* advertisers asking for proof
* future AI answering prospects

This also sets up the future AI answering service.

### 18. Category Exclusivity

Category exclusivity is a premium.

Basic version:

Only one business in a category appears on a specific edition.

Examples:

* one roofer
* one plumber
* one electrician
* one Realtor
* one dentist
* one HVAC company

Positioning:

**“I’m limiting the card so it does not become cluttered with direct competitors. If you want to be the featured business in your category, you can lock that category for the edition.”**

Exclusivity can be:

* per edition
* per ZIP
* per city
* annual

Exclusivity should not be given away casually. It reduces inventory.

### 19. ZIP / Route Lock

ZIP or route lock allows an advertiser to secure presence in a specific area.

Examples:

* appear in every Catonsville mailing
* lock 21228
* appear on both 10,000-home postcard route groups
* target higher-income carrier routes
* target routes near business location

Positioning:

**“If a specific area matters to your business, we can reserve that area for you instead of rotating you in and out.”**

This should cost more because it limits flexibility.

### 20. Multi-Card Bundle

If running multiple 2,500-household cards, larger advertisers can buy across multiple cards.

Example:

* Catonsville East + Catonsville West
* Catonsville + Arbutus
* Catonsville + Ellicott City
* two 10,000-home postcard editions = roughly 10,000 local households

Positioning:

**“If you want the wider reach, we can place you on both cards.”**

This is cleaner than creating a separate “5,000 option” if the system is built around multiple 10,000-home postcard editions.

Each placement should be tracked separately with its own QR short code.

### 23. Handling Common Objections

#### “I need to think about it.”

Response:

**“That’s fair. Just so you know, I’m limiting spots by category and the spot is not reserved until payment is in. If your category fills, I won’t put a direct competitor next to you.”**

#### “It’s too expensive.”

Response:

**“I understand. The print spot includes the mailing exposure, the digital landing page, QR code, and your local offer online. If print is too much for this round, I can also do a lower-cost digital-only listing.”**

#### “Does this guarantee leads?”

Response:

**“No, I’m not going to promise a specific number of leads. What I can promise is the placement, the mailing, the digital page, and the tracking we can report. The goal is visibility, local awareness, and making it easy for people to contact you.”**

#### “Do people read mail anymore?”

Response:

**“A lot of advertising has moved online, which is exactly why a clean physical piece can stand out. This also is not print only — the QR connects people to your digital page and offer.”**

#### “I already advertise somewhere else.”

Response:

**“That’s usually a good sign. This is not meant to replace everything else. It’s a local print-and-digital placement focused on this specific area.”**

#### “Can I just be online?”

Response:

**“Yes. The digital-only listing is a lower monthly option. You still get the landing page and offer, just without the mailed card placement.”**

#### “Can I pay after it mails?”

Response:

**“For print spots, payment has to be upfront because the card has hard print and postage costs. Once payment is in, your spot is reserved.”**

### 24. Sales Qualification Questions

Ask simple questions:

* Are you trying to reach homeowners in this area?
* Do you currently advertise locally?
* Do you have a seasonal offer right now?
* What service or product do you most want to promote?
* Do most of your customers come from this area?
* Would you want to be on one edition or stay visible every mailing?
* Do you care about a specific ZIP or route area?
* Would you want a smaller test spot or a larger premium placement?
* If print is not right this time, would a digital listing make sense?

The goal is not to interrogate them.

The goal is to place them into the right offer.

### 26. Renewal Strategy

Renewals should be built into the sales process from the first conversation.

When selling the first spot, mention:

**“We’re planning to mail roughly every six weeks, so if this is a fit, you’ll have the option to continue into future editions.”**

After mailing, follow up:

**“I’m starting the next edition and wanted to give you first option to keep your spot before I open the category back up.”**

If they do not renew print:

Offer digital-only.

**“No problem. We can keep your landing page and offer active online for a lower monthly rate.”**

If they renew:

Offer prepaid package.

**“If you already know you want to stay visible, we can lock you in for the next three mailings and include a Pick of the Week feature.”**

### 28. Payment Policy

Payment should be simple and firm.

Print placements:

* paid upfront
* no spot reserved until paid
* payment due before design begins
* payment due before print deadline
* no advertiser should delay the mailing

Digital-only:

* monthly recurring
* cancelable with notice
* page removed or downgraded if payment stops

Pick of the Week:

* paid before scheduled feature date
* date not reserved until paid

Call tracking:

* monthly recurring
* number can be disabled or reassigned after cancellation
* any recording requires proper disclosure/consent handling

Prepaid packages:

* paid upfront or through automatic billing
* define what happens if advertiser wants to pause
* define whether unused mailings expire
* define whether placement can be transferred to another city/card

### 29. What Not to Sell Too Early

Avoid over-selling advanced services before the core card is validated.

Do not lead with:

* AI answering
* complicated dashboards
* custom websites
* advanced analytics
* multi-city sponsorships
* ad agency retainers
* large custom tech promises

Lead with:

* local card
* local households
* digital page included
* QR code
* simple offer
* limited spots

Then upsell later.

### 30. First Edition Sales Rule

The first edition should prove demand.

Minimum sales goal before mailing:

* enough paid placements to cover print/postage
* enough margin to justify labor
* enough category mix to make the card useful
* at least one strong household-friendly offer

Do not mail a weak card just because it was planned.

Do not delay forever trying to perfect it either.

A good first edition is:

* paid
* clean
* useful
* mailed
* followed up
* improved on the second edition

### 31. Core Sales Principle

The offer should be easy to understand:

**“Get featured locally in print and online.”**

Everything else supports that.

If the sales explanation becomes too complicated, simplify it.

The business owner should quickly understand:

* where it goes
* what they get
* what it costs
* how to pay
* when it mails
* what happens after
* how they renew

Maryland Local Picks wins by making local advertising simple for business owners and useful for residents.
