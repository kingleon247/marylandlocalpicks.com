# Maryland Local Picks — Sales Scripts & Objection Handling

## Page 7: Walk-In Pitch, Phone Script, Follow-Up, Downsell, Renewal, and Objections

### 1. Purpose

This document gives Maryland Local Picks a practical sales language library.

The goal is not to sound scripted.

The goal is to have simple, clear language ready for:

- walk-ins
- phone calls
- emails
- texts
- follow-ups
- objections
- renewals
- digital-only downsells
- Pick of the Week pitches

The tone should be local, direct, relaxed, and credible.

Avoid sounding like a generic marketing agency.

### 2. Core One-Line Explanation

Use this whenever someone asks what it is.

**"Maryland Local Picks sells premium shared 9x12 postcard advertising mailed to 10,000 local households. Only one business per category is accepted whenever possible."**

City version:

**"Catonsville Local Picks features local businesses on a premium shared 9x12 postcard mailed directly to 10,000 households in the area."**

### 3. 15-Second Walk-In Opener

**“Hey, I’m putting together Catonsville Local Picks — it’s a local printed card and digital guide featuring selected businesses around Catonsville. I’m talking to a few local owners now and wanted to see if this might be a fit for you.”**

Alternative:

**“I’m another local business owner putting together a Catonsville card that goes to local households. It also includes a digital page for each business. I’m keeping it limited by category so it doesn’t turn into a cluttered coupon sheet.”**

### 4. 30-Second Pitch

**“The idea is simple. We’re mailing a clean local card to Catonsville households featuring selected local businesses and offers. Each paid spot also gets a digital landing page on Maryland Local Picks, so people can scan the QR code, view your offer, and call, email, or get directions from their phone. I’m trying to keep it curated and useful — not just a pile of random ads.”**

### 5. Phone Opener

**“Hey, this is [Name] with Maryland Local Picks. I’m putting together the Catonsville Local Picks card and digital guide, featuring selected local businesses going out to households in the area. I was reaching out because your business looked like it could be a good fit. Are you the right person to talk to about local advertising?”**

If yes:

**“Great. It’s a print-and-digital placement. You’d get a spot on the mailed card, plus a local landing page and QR code so people can view your offer and contact you online. I’m keeping spots limited by category.”**

### 6. Email Intro

Subject ideas:

- Catonsville Local Picks
- Local print + digital feature for Catonsville
- Quick question about featuring [Business Name]
- Catonsville card going to local households

Email:

**Hi [Name],**

**I’m putting together Catonsville Local Picks — a printed local card and digital guide featuring selected businesses, offers, and places worth knowing around Catonsville.**

**Each paid print spot also includes a digital landing page, QR code, city-page listing, and space for an offer or call-to-action.**

**I thought [Business Name] could be a good fit for the [category] spot.**

**Would you be open to taking a quick look?**

**— [Name]**

### 7. Follow-Up After Interest

**“Just following up on Catonsville Local Picks. I’m still filling the first edition and wanted to see if you wanted me to hold a spot for [Business Name]. The spot is only reserved once payment is in, and I’m trying not to put direct competitors next to each other.”**

### 8. Payment Link Message

**“Great — here’s the payment link to reserve your spot: [link]. Once that’s complete, I’ll send the info form for your logo, photos, offer, and business details. After I build your ad/page, I’ll send you a proof before anything goes to print.”**

### 9. Asset Collection Message

**“Thanks again for being part of Catonsville Local Picks. Here’s the quick info form for your ad and digital page: [link]. Send the best logo/photos you have, your main phone number, website, services, and any offer you want featured. I’ll send a proof before print.”**

### 10. Digital Page Included Pitch

**“The digital page is included so your ad doesn’t disappear after the mail hits. People can scan the QR code and come back to your page later to call, email, get directions, or view your offer.”**

### 11. Pick of the Week Pitch

**“We’re also doing Pick of the Week features between mailings. It’s a way to keep your offer visible on the Catonsville page and give us something current to promote online. It can be a standalone feature or included as a bonus with a multi-mailing package.”**

### 12. Social Promotion Pitch

**“If you want more visibility around the offer, we can also promote the Pick of the Week through our local social channels and link people back to your landing page.”**

### 13. Digital-Only Downsell

Use when print is too expensive or the business is not ready.

**“No problem. If the print card isn’t the right fit right now, we can still feature you digitally with a business landing page, city-page listing, and active offer for a lower monthly rate. That keeps you visible online without paying for the mailed card.”**

### 14. Renewal Script

**"I'm filling the next 10,000-home mailing and wanted to give you first option to keep your category before I open it back up. Spots are limited and only one business per category is accepted whenever possible."**

### 15. Exposure-Not-Leads Script

**"Maryland Local Picks sells local exposure and visibility. We don't promise a specific number of calls, leads, sales, or redemptions. Results depend on your offer, timing, and follow-up."**

### 16. Call Tracking Add-On Script

**“Basic pages track call-button taps. If you want real call reporting, we can add a dedicated SignalWire tracking number that forwards to your business and logs call times and duration. That’s more of a premium add-on, but it gives stronger proof.”**

### 17. Objection: “I need to think about it.”

**“That’s fair. Just so you know, the spot isn’t reserved until payment is in. I’m limiting categories, so if your category fills, I won’t put a direct competitor next to you. Want me to send the info so you can look it over?”**

### 18. Objection: “It’s too expensive.”

**“I get it. The print spot includes the mailed exposure, digital landing page, QR code, and your offer online. If the print placement is too much for this round, the lower-cost option is digital-only, where we keep your page and offer live without the mailed card.”**

### 19. Objection: "Does this guarantee leads?"

**"No. Maryland Local Picks sells local exposure and visibility. We don't promise a specific number of calls, leads, sales, or redemptions. What we deliver is category-exclusive placement on a premium 9x12 postcard mailed to 10,000 local households, plus ad layout help."**

### 20. Objection: “Do people read mail anymore?”

**“A lot of advertising has moved online, which is part of why a clean physical piece can stand out. And this isn’t print only — the QR code connects people to your digital page and offer.”**

### 21. Objection: “I already advertise somewhere else.”

**“That’s usually a good sign because it means you already understand local advertising. This isn’t meant to replace everything else. It’s a local print-and-digital placement focused on this specific area.”**

### 22. Objection: “Can I pay after it mails?”

**“For print spots, payment has to be upfront because the card has hard print and postage costs. Once payment is in, your spot is reserved and I can start building the ad and digital page.”**

### 23. Objection: “I don’t have a coupon.”

**“That’s fine. It doesn’t have to be a big discount. It can be a free estimate, seasonal special, new customer offer, consultation, or simply a clear call-to-action. The main thing is giving people a reason to scan or call.”**

### 24. Objection: “We don’t need more business right now.”

**“Makes sense. For some businesses this is less about immediate overflow and more about staying visible locally before they need you. If timing is bad for print, I can check back for the next edition or keep you in mind for a digital feature.”**

### 25. Objection: “Send me information.”

**“Absolutely. I’ll send a quick overview. Just so I send the right thing, are you more interested in the mailed card, the digital listing, or possibly being featured as Pick of the Week?”**

### 26. Closing Question

Use simple closes.

**“Do you want to grab one of the available spots for this first Catonsville edition?”**

**“Would a half spot or standard spot make more sense to start?”**

**“Do you want to try one edition first, or would you rather lock in a few mailings?”**

**“If print is too much right now, should I set you up with the digital-only option?”**

### 27. Sales Rule

Do not argue people into buying.

Explain it clearly, show the value, ask for the spot, and follow up.

The goal is to find business owners who already understand visibility and local marketing.

The easiest buyers are usually already advertising somewhere.
